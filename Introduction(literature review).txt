Drug abuse globally emerged as a significant Public health issue impacting the youth and university students profoundly. Recent research highlights adolescence and early youth as the most crucial age periods to initiate drug use, highlighting the necessity of an effective preventive communication strategy to target the young demographic population (UNODC, 2024; Rutherford et al., 2022).

Previous studies indicate that message credibility and participatory communication are equally important as information dissemination to create anti-drug awareness. In this regard, health awareness on social media channels opens an opportunity to exchange mutual experiences and awareness to quickly disseminate the information among young people.
However, some studies underscore that misleading content about drug abuse can also spread through these social media channels, underscoring the understanding of the ability of evidence-based messages and appropriate communication strategies (Agberotimi et al., 2025; Rutherford et al., 2022).

On the other hand, research conducted at the university level on the anti-drug awareness campaign shows that digital communication and mass media are important factors in increasing student awareness and redefining students' behaviour. At the same level, this study highlights the theoretical necessity of student engagement and communication sources (SAGE Open, 2025; UNODC, 2024).
